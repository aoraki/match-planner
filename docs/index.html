💡 Things to Consider When Planning Subs

  • 🔀
    Position flexibility — The more positions each player can cover, the more substitution options you have. A player who can play both DEF and MID is far more useful as a sub than one who can only play MID.
  • ⭐
    Ability rating per position — A player might be ★★★★ at DEF but only ★★ at MID. The planner uses these per-position ratings to avoid swaps that significantly weaken your team at a specific position.
  • 🏃
    Stamina / fitness level — Midfielders cover the most ground and tire fastest. Players with lower stamina should be rotated more frequently. Mark this in the Squad tab so the planner can prioritise resting them.
  • 🥅
    Goalkeeper rarely rotates — Unless you have two capable goalkeepers, the GK should play the full game. Use the "Protect Goalkeeper" setting. Only rotate if your backup GK is also confident in goal.
  • 🕐
    Half-time is your best sub window — You can make all substitutions at once without disrupting play. Use it to bring on multiple players simultaneously and re-organise your team shape.
  • 📊
    Minimum guaranteed playing time — Decide before the game: "everyone plays at least X minutes." This is important for squad morale, player development, and in many youth leagues it's a rule. 10–15 mins is a reasonable minimum for casual leagues.
  • 🏆
    Score situation — The planner can't react to the score. If you're winning by 2 with 10 minutes left, you can afford to bring on weaker or less-played players. If it's a tight game you may want to keep your stronger players on. Always override the plan when the game situation demands it.
  • 📅
    Season fairness — A player who played 45 minutes last week should perhaps be prioritised this week even if the game-day plan would otherwise not start them. Use the Season Tracking tab to monitor cumulative minutes.
  • 🔄
    Rolling substitutions (re-entry) — Most 7-a-side youth leagues allow players to come back on after being substituted. This gives you far more flexibility and is the default setting. Disable this if your league doesn't allow it.
  • 🌡️
    Weather and conditions — Hot or humid weather increases fatigue significantly. Consider shorter sub intervals (every 7–8 mins) in hot weather so no player is overworked.
  • 🩹
    Same-day injuries or illness — Players may arrive feeling unwell or carrying a knock. Before generating a plan, remove anyone from the squad who you suspect may need to come off early, or mark them as a low-stamina player.
  • 📍
    Preferred vs. forced positions — Some players have a clear preferred position (e.g. always wants to play ATT). Consider their preference when assigning positions. Making a player play out of position repeatedly can affect their enjoyment and development.
  • ⏱️
    Minimum time before re-subbing — Don't sub a player on and then off again within a minute or two. The planner enforces a minimum time on field before a player can be subbed off again (default 10 mins). This avoids disrupting rhythm and is respectful to the players.
  • 🧠
    Know the plan before the game — Show the substitution schedule to your assistant or a parent helper before kick-off so someone else can track the time and remind you when to make changes. You'll be focused on coaching, not watching the clock.
  • 🔧
    Override freely — This planner is a starting point, not a rigid script. If a player is having a great game or another is struggling, ignore the plan and make the call. Trust your judgement.

📐 How the Algorithm Works

The planner divides the game into segments based on your sub interval setting (plus half-time). It calculates a target playing time per player: (7 slots × game minutes) ÷ squad size. At each break, it looks for players on the bench who are furthest below their target and players on the field furthest above theirs, matching them by position compatibility. A quality penalty prevents automatically swapping a ★★★★ player for a ★★ replacement. Urgent bench players (who won't reach even 50% of their target if not brought on soon) are priority-flagged and will be subbed in even if the on-field player is slightly below target.
